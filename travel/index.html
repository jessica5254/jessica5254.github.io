St. Augustine,  Florida

The oldest, most charming city in the United States!

Welcome to
St. Augustine!

This welcoming, historic, vibrant city has so much to offer! Right on the Florida coast, the views, piers, and beaches are only the start of what this city has to provide. Take a stroll along the pier, peruse the shops along the famous St. George street, and enjoy the deep rooted history our lovely city has to share!

Rich History

Settled in the year 1565 by the Spanish, St. Augustine oldest city in the United States that is still lively and occupied. Originally, the city was named San Agustín by the discoverer Pedro Menéndez de Avilés, after the saint who's feast was on the day of discovering the land.

Key Locations

St. Augustine's deep routed history is well preserved and on display! Take guided tours or simply take a stroll, around every corner is a new discovery, with many plaques and signs providing guidance and insight into the history of the city. Some of the main landmarks include a still standing Spanish fort from the 18th century, the Castillo de San Marcos on the bay, the Lighthouse and Maritime Museum, the breathtaking Cathedral Bascilla old fort, or even the famous Fountain of Youth.

Entertainment

From the Aligator Farm, party boats on the water to the Ripley's Belive it or Not Museum, St. Augustine has a plethora of activities and events! The beaches, piers and boardwalks make for beautiful site seeing, or rent a boat and get on the water! Or, if you are looking for something a little darker, checkout the haunted night tours! Taking you to some of the most historic, and haunted, locations while providing their historic significance and the stories of the lives of those of the past.

Shopping and Dining

St. Augustine has many locally owned shops and restraunts. Take a visit to St. George Street and let yourself get lost in the culture and aesthetic. This historic street, not to far from the coast, is lined with historic locations to visit, as well as local owned shops, offering a great variety of novelty items, local treasures, and is an overall reflection of the diversity of what this city has to offer, old and new! The cobblestone, pedestrian only street and alleys are a great area to explore, catering to every interest!
